Sam Altman (born April 22, 1985) is an American entrepreneur, investor, and the current CEO of OpenAI. He was the president of Y Combinator, one of the most successful startup accelerators in Silicon Valley. Altman has been a prominent figure in the tech industry, known for his role in shaping the startup ecosystem and his work in artificial intelligence.

Born: April 22, 1985

Key Events in Sam Altman's Life:

  1. Co-founded Loopt in 2005, a location-based social networking service (acquired by Green Dot Corporation).
  2. Joined Y Combinator as president in 2014 and helped fund many successful companies.
  3. Co-founded OpenAI in 2015 with Elon Musk and others to ensure that artificial intelligence benefits humanity.
  4. In 2019, Sam Altman became the CEO of OpenAI and has continued to lead the charge in AI research and development.

Key Inventions & Contributions:

  • Y Combinator – Revolutionized the startup accelerator model and helped launch companies like Dropbox, Airbnb, and Stripe.
  • OpenAI – Pioneering research in artificial intelligence, especially in developing large language models like GPT-3.
  • Loopt – Pioneered location-based social media services and was acquired in 2012.
